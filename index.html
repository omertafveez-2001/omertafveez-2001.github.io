About Me

Hi, I am Omer Tafveez. I am currently pursuing my Masters in Information Science at the University of Michigan-Ann Arbor, where I am focusing on AI research. I completed my Bachelors in Economics-Mathematics and Computer Science from Lahore University of Management Sciences, specialising in Machine Learning.
I have a research experience in Model Compression, Algorithmic Optimization in ML, and Mechanistic Interpretability . For my senior thesis, I investigated methods to improve Grouped Query Attention using key-norm information and proposed a Decoupled Gradient Knowledge Distillation approach that enhances the transfer of dark knowledge via gradient signals from target and non-target logits.
During my undergrad, I also got to work as a Data Science Intern at Lipton, and Toyota where I worked on deploying route-scheduling engine for sales and time-series forecasting respectively.
Currently, I am interested at the intersection of AI Safety, Reasoning, and Interpretability.
