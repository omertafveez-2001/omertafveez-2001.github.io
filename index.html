Personal Project

Transformers for Timeseries: A Statistical View

Evaluated Autoformer on time-series data beyond accuracy by analyzing structural breakpoints. While it predicted the correct number of breaks, the intervals were misaligned and clustered—indicating instability. Used trust scores based on normality, homoscedasticity, and autocorrelation to assess statistical reliability.

Research Experience

Nov 2024 - Feb 2025

Research Assistant - Project Lead

City @LUMS, Project: Decoupled Gradient Knowledge Distillation

  • Engineered gradient-based decoupled distillation that maximizes MSE between the gradients of target and non-target class losses,
    enhancing orthogonal learning and functional gradient independence.
  • The method acts as a regularizer, mitigating overconfidence, encouraging fidelity, and accelerating feature compaction.
  • Achieved 6% increase and up to 15% gain on OOD data over standard decoupled distillation across ViT, MobileNet, ResNet, and VGG.

Research Assistant - Project Lead

Center of Speech and Language Technologies, Project: Key-Driven Grouped Query Attention

  • Built “Dynamic Grouped Query Attention” with window-based norm updates, assigning queries to keys by changes in key norms.
  • Implemented EMA-based key-norm evolution, smoothing rapid changes and reducing noise, leading to a +3% accuracy gain
    on ViT-Large across CIFAR-100, TinyImageNet, and Food101.
  • Further evaluated on GPT-NEOx, GPT-2, and T5.
