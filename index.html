About Me

I'm a dedicated Machine Learning Engineer who loves building intelligent systems. My journey into machine learning started with a deep curiosity: why could I interpret a simple linear regression model so easily, but not a deep neural network? That question sparked an obsession with model interpretability — and soon led me down a path of reading dozens of research papers on explainability, model compression, optimization, transformer architectures, reasoning, and AI safety.

Over the years, I've gained hands-on experience in various aspects of the ML literature, from Grouped Query Attention and Sparse Autoencoders to Knowledge Distillation, Evaluation, and Agents. I'm proficient in a range of tools and frameworks, always eager to learn new technologies and apply them to real-world challenges.

Outside of work, I enjoy binge-watching Netflix and Prime, Acrylic Painting, and photography.
