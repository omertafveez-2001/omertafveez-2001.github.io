Sparse-Autoencoders Guided FitNets

Muhammad Khaquan, Omer Tafveez, Dr.Muhammad Tahir Lahore University of Management Sciences

We introduce Sparse Autoencoder-Guided FitNets (SAE-FitNets), an enhanced student-teacher framework that integrates sparse autoencoders to refine and inject teacher feature representations through decoder-weighted feature alignment. This method promotes representational sparsity, stabilizes intermediate supervision, and enhances transfer fidelity. SAE-FitNets achieve a +2% improvement in-distribution and a +11% gain on Out-of-Distribution (OOD) datasets including Style-Transfer Animals100 and TinyImagenette, demonstrating superior generalization and compact feature learning over traditional FitNet baselines.

Decoupled Gradient Knowledge Distillation for OOD Generalization

Omer Tafveez, Muhammad Khaquan, Advisor: Dr. Muhammad Tahir Lahore University of Management Sciences

We propose Decoupled Gradient Knowledge Distillation, a novel knowledge distillation technique that enforces functional gradient orthogonality between target and non-target classes in the student model. This method significantly improves teacher fidelity, enhances neural collapse between feature representations, Out-of-Distribution (OOD) generalization, and penalizes overconfident predictions , achieving state-of-the-art results on TinyImagenette, Animals100, and CIFAR-100. This approach attained 6% on standard datasets and 15% improvement on OOD datasets over traditional DKD methods.

Key-Driven Grouped Query Attention

Zohaib Khan, Omer Tafveez, Muhammad Khaquan, Dr.Muhammad Tahir Lahore University of Management Sciences

We propose Key-Driven Grouped Query Attention (DGQA), a dynamic attention mechanism that assigns query groups based on key-norm evolution, enabling more efficient and adaptive token-to-token interactions. By incorporating Exponential Moving Average (EMA) smoothing over key-norm changes, DGQA mitigates attention noise and stabilizes inter-group communication. This approach yields a +3% accuracy improvement on ViT-Large across CIFAR-100, TinyImageNet, and Food101 , with additional evaluations on GPT-NeoX, GPT-2, and T5. The training pipeline integrates mixed-precision, cache-line optimization, and gradient checkpointing to enhance scalability and computational efficiency.

Trustworthy Time-Series Forecasting with Transformer Models

Evaluated Autoformer on time-series data beyond accuracy by analyzing structural breakpoints. While it predicted the correct number of breaks, the intervals were misaligned and clustered—indicating instability. Used trust scores based on normality, homoscedasticity, and autocorrelation to assess statistical reliability.
